Your Non-Negotiables (minimum 3)
Know Your
Enemy
Before Day 1, understand what you're up against.
Resistance is the internal pushback you feel the moment you try to move forward.
It doesn't show up when you're coasting. It shows up right before growth — disguised as excuses, delay, distraction, and self-doubt.
"Resistance is not a problem to solve. It is the threshold every meaningful change requires you to cross."
HOW IT SHOWS UP
"I'll do it later."
"I'm too tired today."
"I'll restart when things calm down."
"Missing one day won't matter."
Every one of those feels like logic. None of them are.
DON'T ARGUE WITH EXCUSES — REPLACE THEM
The ExcuseThe Response
"I don't have time"Do the smallest possible version. Start anyway.
"I'm too tired"Do it tired. Lower intensity — never consistency.
"I already missed a day"Continue immediately. No catching up. No punishment.
"I don't feel motivated"Motivation is optional. Action is not.
Burn
the Boats
You are not entering this casually. You are removing the option to retreat.

By starting this program, I acknowledge that:


Every task must be completed as written. No substitutions, no modifications, no delays.


Partial completion is failure.


Consequences are real. Miss 1 day: document it and add to end of week. Miss 2 days: complete a penalty challenge before continuing. Miss 3+ days or fall below the weekly threshold: restart the current week from Day 1.


Discomfort is expected. Resistance is expected. Neither is an excuse.


Once I begin, there is no negotiating with who I used to be.
